Instructions

Drag the variable names from the first column to the column/row to the right to select the variables whose cross tabulation you want to compute. Note that you can drag multiple variables to create a multilevel variable. Use the leftmost dropdown menu to change the way the result is displayed, and the menu to the right to choose the aggregation function.

Notes

  • All figures are in 2016 dollars.
  • The Survey of Consumer Finances is a survey of households. Individual data such as age, race and education level refers to the head of the household.
  • The full data set is available for download from the Federal Reserve Board's website.
  • Due to the survey design, the row weights are not integers, and thus it is possible to get fractional results for features that are usually integral.
  • The aggregator Count returns the weighted count, i.e. the estimated count in the population. The aggregator Sample Count gives the count in the sample before weighting/replication.
